About Me

I am an astronomer currently at the University of Missouri—Kansas City, finishing my doctoral work and scheduled to graduate in spring of 2022. I am a part of the Massive and Distant Clusters of WISE Survey (MaDCoWS) collaboration, studying high-redshift infrared-selected galaxy clusters, including some of the most massive clusters in the universe at redshift z~1. I have been studying the evolution of clusters at this stage of the universe using measurements of the total mass and stellar mass of the clusters. Through analysis of the stellar mass fractions and the luminosity functions of MaDCoWS clusters, I have found that there is not a significant difference in composition between infrared-selected and ICM-selected clusters (though merging clusters complicate this) and that the MaDCoWS clusters appear to be evolving passively.

As part of my research, I have also been heavily involved with data reduction for MaDCoWS. I have built the pipelines to reduce Gemini GMOS imaging (through multiple iterations as their software has changed) and have produced multi-wavelength catalogues from GMOS and Spitzer/IRAC images. I have also reduced Sunyaev Zel'dovich data from the now-defunct Combined Array for Research in Millimeter-wave Astronomy (CARMA) and been involved in reducing data from the Alma Atacama Compact Array (ACA).



I'm originally from the Kansas City area and I got my Bachelor's degree in physics from Truman State University in Kirksville, MO. Although it is very hard to find a suitably dark sky in Kansas City, I enjoy talking about astronomy and host occasional outreach events. Most of my Twitter rants and rambles are about one of these sides, though I do also try to use it for some outreach, particularly pertaining to my work.
